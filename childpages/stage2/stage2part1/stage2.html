Natural languages are very ambigous. Computers need clear instructions. Many words do not often mean more clarity, they mean more mess, or trying to figure a way out from the one created. And so computer lanuages have rules to make it clearfor computers.

Constructing "sentences"

The grammar of a language defines a sentence through a construct of type-elements. (Subject - Verb - Object) They can be replaced by elements that are part of their own group. The repleacable elements are called non-terminal. They can be replaced/derived by other non-terminals, or by non-repleacable elments: terminals, depending on the rules of the language. Once all parts of our "sentence" have reached the terminals, we have finished constructing the sentence.

It's the Backus Naur Form.
